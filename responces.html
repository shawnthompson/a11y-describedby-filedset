1. What does "NOC" stand for?

  • National Occupational Code
  • National Occupations of Canada
  • National Occupational Classification
  • National Occupational Clarification

Explanation

The NOC is a way of "classifying" occupations according to skill level and skill type. The correct answer is National Occupational Classification.

2. Who is MOST likely to use the NOC resources?

  • Immigration officer
  • Clerical Assistant
  • Pharmacist
  • None of the above

Explanation

Employment Counsellors throughout Canada depend on the NOC to provide their clients with occupational information.

3. The NOC can be used for which of the following:

  • Collection of labour market information
  • Analysis of occupational data
  • Making career decisions
  • All of the above

Explanation

The NOC is used by various clients to perform all of these activities.

4. A NOC unit group code is composed of how many digits?

  • Three
  • Four
  • Two
  • Five

Explanation

The NOC unit group which represents the occupation level, is composed of four digits.

5. For all occupations, the first digit of a NOC represents:

  • Skill Level
  • Skill Type
  • Major Group
  • Essential Skill

Explanation

Skill type is represented by the first digit of a NOC code.

6. For non-management occupations, the second digit of a NOC represents:

  • Skill Level
  • Skill Type
  • Major Group
  • Essential Skill

Explanation

Skill level is represented by the second digit in a NOC code.

7. The NOC has how many skill type categories?

  • 10
  • 8
  • 9
  • 11

Explanation

The NOC includes skill types 0 - through 9 and therefore there are 10 different skill types.

8. How many occupational groups are described in the NOC?

  • 500
  • 40
  • 140
  • Approximately 40 000

Explanation

There are 500 NOC groups in the NOC 2011 as well as 2016 and 520 groups in the 2006 version of the NOC.

9. How many skill levels appear in the NOC?

  • 2
  • 3
  • 4
  • 5

Explanation

There are a total of four skill levels in the NOC and are labelled A through D.

10. Which of the following is responsible for the production of the NOC?

  • Municipal Governments
  • Private Industry
  • Sectoral Partnerships
  • Government of Canada

Explanation

The Federal government of Canada is responsible for the production of the NOC.

11. In what year was the NOC implemented?

  • 1945
  • 1992
  • 1988
  • 1975

Explanation

The first version of the NOC was introduced in 1992.

12. What best describes skill level?

  • Domain of work
  • Training and/or education
  • Both of the above
  • None of the above

Explanation

Skill level is best described by training and/or education. Domain of work refers to skill type.

13. What is the main purpose of the NOC?

  • Provides a standardized language for describing the work of Canadians.
  • Provides a standardized language for describing industries in Canada.
  • Both of the above
  • None of the above

Explanation

The NOC is designed to provide a standard way of describing the work of Canadians.

14. Which of the following use(s) the NOC:

  • National JobBank
  • Working in Canada Tool
  • Both of the above
  • None of the above

Explanation

Both the National JobBank and Working in Canada Tool use the NOC as the tool for coding and describing occupations.

15. A NOC code that starts with a "0" represents:

  • Management Occupations
  • Health Occupations
  • Both of the above
  • None of the above

Explanation

Any NOC code that begins with "0" represents the Management Occupations skill type.
